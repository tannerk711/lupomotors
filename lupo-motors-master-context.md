## Business Overview

### What Lupo Motors Does
Lupo Motors operates as a **wholesale car buying middleman** — connecting individual car sellers directly with a network of dealership buyers. The owner (referred to as "Drake" or "Mike" in conversations) doesn't actually take possession of cars; he facilitates transactions between sellers and his established network of dealer buyers across the country.

**The Core Model:**
- Sellers submit their car info (VIN, mileage, photos)
- Drake/Mike gets an instant offer from his buyer network using tools like AccuTrade
- He adds his margin and presents the offer to the seller
- If accepted, the buyer picks up the car and Drake gets paid
- Transaction complete — often in minutes, not days

### Company History & Evolution
Lupo Motors has evolved significantly over time:
1. **Started** as a specialty automotive shop (detailing, wraps, mechanics)
2. **Built for 10+ years** a massive network of dealer relationships
3. **Pivoted** to pure wholesaling after realizing the model was lower risk, higher margin, less time
4. **Recently sold** the physical shop operations to Park Place
5. **Now operates** as a lean, relationship-driven wholesaling operation

> "I spent 10 years building this huge network. I haven't, like, done anything with it... I was sick, so I took a month and see how much I can do. Dude, I sold like 35 cars. I made 50 grand in three weeks."

## How the Business Actually Works

### The Transaction Flow

**Step 1: Lead Comes In**
- Seller submits VIN + mileage (minimum requirements)
- Helpful additions: asking price, lien status, photos, modifications

**Step 2: Instant Valuation**
- Drake plugs VIN into AccuTrade (dealer tool, $3k/month — he borrows a login)
- Gets instant wholesale value and retail estimate
- Can get automatic offers through the system immediately

**Step 3: Offer Made**
- Normal cars (Toyota, F-350, etc.): Offer in 2-5 minutes
- Exotic/optioned cars (Urus, etc.): May need more details, up to 1 hour
- Offer presented to seller with margin built in

**Step 4: Deal Acceptance**
- If local: Can meet within 1-2 hours, cashier's check in hand
- If remote: 24-48 hours for inspection + wire transfer
- Transaction complete within 2-3 days max

**Step 5: Payment Mechanics**
- Drake gets paid when title is delivered to buyer
- Most buyers pay within 24-48 hours of receiving title
- Some trusted buyers will pay before title (for expensive cars)
- Drake sometimes "floats" money for 30-60 days on financed vehicles

### The Money Flow
> "Most of the time, I get paid within 24 to 48 hours, and I use their money to buy the car. I don't even use my own money."

The only time Drake uses his own capital is when floating payoffs on financed vehicles — and he won't do that for small margins:
> "I'm not gonna be out my 100 grand for two months to make a thousand dollars."

### Profit Margins
- Typical deal: $833 - $5,000+ per car
- High-option exotics: Can be $10,000-$20,000
- Volume goal: 20-35+ cars per month
- Monthly revenue potential: $50,000+ working 3-5 hours/day

## What Cars Lupo Buys

### Target Vehicles
**Price Range:** $10,000 - $150,000 (updated from original $5K-$300K)

**Condition Requirements:**
- Clean CarFax (no accidents, dings, title issues)
- Running and drivable
- Nice, quality vehicles — "works, runs, not broken down"

**Examples of GOOD leads:**
- Ford Raptor (clean, maybe high mileage but nice)
- Escalade (clean, never been in an accident)
- Rivian ($60K range)
- WRX/STI (100K miles, mostly stock, maybe exhaust and tune)
- Any Toyota, Honda, Ford truck that's clean
- Exotics: Urus, G-Wagon, Ferrari, Lamborghini, Porsche

**Examples of BAD leads:**
- 1970s VW Beetle with holes in the floor
- Clapped-out, modified cars with 300K miles
- Cars with CarFax dings (accidents, salvage titles)
- Cars that need significant repairs
- Underwater loans (owe more than it's worth)

> "We want to definitely kick out probably the bottom 50% that's going to come in."

### Special Cases
- **Classic project cars:** Has ONE buyer for unlimited project classics
- **Exotics with options:** Need detailed options list (can add $40K+ to value)
- **Financed vehicles:** Okay, but affects timeline and margin requirements

## Lead Qualification Requirements

### Minimum Info Needed
1. **VIN** (most important — decodes everything)
2. **Mileage**

### Helpful Additional Info
- Asking price (prevents wasted time)
- Lien status (owned outright vs. financed)
- Modifications (yes/no)
- Trim level (for optioned vehicles)
- Photos (5 is ideal)
- Window sticker or options list (for exotics)

### Disqualifying Factors
- Underwater on loan (owe more than value)
- Damaged/salvage title history
- Non-running/broken down
- Heavily modified "clapped out" cars
- Unrealistic price expectations

> "If somebody's like, dude, I owe 90 and I can't afford to get out of it, then we're like, okay, well, that's just not possible. You got to go somewhere else, man."
